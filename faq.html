FAQ — Permanent Makeup & Healing

Clear answers about pain level, healing, longevity, and who is a good candidate for permanent makeup.

Before Your Appointment

How to prepare and who is a good candidate for PMU.

Who is a good candidate for permanent makeup?

Most healthy clients without serious uncontrolled conditions are good candidates. PMU works especially well if you:

  • Want to save time on daily makeup
  • Have sparse or asymmetrical brows, pale lips, or invisible lash line
  • Prefer natural enhancements that still look like “you”

We’ll review your medical history and current medications during consultation to make sure it’s safe.

Who should NOT get permanent makeup?

PMU may be not recommended or delayed if you have:

  • Uncontrolled diabetes, bleeding disorders, or immune disorders
  • Active skin conditions in the area: eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis, infection
  • Recent chemical peels, laser, or strong retinoid use in the last 4 weeks
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding (usually recommended to wait)
  • Recent Botox / fillers in the area (we may need to wait 2–4 weeks)

If you’re unsure, we’ll discuss your case individually before booking.

How should I prepare before my appointment?

For the best result and safer healing:

  • Avoid alcohol, ibuprofen, aspirin, and fish oil 24 hours before the procedure
  • No coffee or energy drinks 2–3 hours before (they can increase sensitivity)
  • Do not apply self-tanner, acids, or retinol on the area 1–2 weeks before
  • Come with your usual makeup look saved in photos if you have a preference
Can I work or drive after my appointment?

Yes. The area may be slightly red or swollen for the first few hours, but you can usually return to normal activities the same day. Brows and lash line are especially discreet; lips may be more visible for 1–3 days due to swelling.

Do I need a consultation first?

A consultation is recommended if you:

  • Have previous permanent makeup from another artist
  • Have complex asymmetry or scars in the area
  • Are unsure which technique is right for you (Brows, Lips, Lash Line, Removal)

We can also consult via photos before you book, if you live far or travel.

During the Procedure

Pain level, numbing, and pigments used at PERMA.

How painful is permanent makeup?

Most clients describe it as discomfort, not true pain. We use professional topical numbing and adjust the technique based on your sensitivity. Brows and lash line are usually very tolerable; lips can feel more sensitive but the numbing significantly reduces the sensation.

How long does the procedure take?

Plan for:

  • Brows: 2.5–3 hours total (mapping + procedure + photos)
  • Lip Blush: 3–3.5 hours
  • Lash Line: 2–2.5 hours
  • Correction / cover-up: timing depends on complexity
What pigments do you use?

We work with high-quality, professional PMU pigments designed for long-term stability and safe healing. Pigments are selected individually based on your skin undertone, hair color, and desired result — always aiming for soft, natural-healed shades rather than harsh, dramatic tones.

Will my brows / lips look very dark right after?

Yes, the color is always more intense the first 2–3 days. As the skin heals and a thin layer exfoliates, the color softens by about 30–50%. We design the tone with this in mind so that the healed result looks natural and balanced.

Can you fix or cover old permanent makeup?

In many cases — yes. We work with:

  • Softening or neutralizing unwanted tones (grey, blue, red)
  • Redesigning shape when possible
  • Combining PMU with professional removal sessions if the pigment is too dark or saturated

A photo or in-person consultation is required to decide the safest plan.

Healing & Longevity

What to expect in the first days and what happens to pigment over the years.

What is the healing process like?

Typical healing timeline (may vary by skin type):

  • Day 1–2: area looks more defined and darker, possible mild swelling
  • Day 3–5: light flaking or peeling, do not pick or scratch
  • Day 7–10: color may look lighter or “too soft” for a short period
  • Week 3–6: pigment settles and becomes more visible and even

Final healed result is evaluated around 6–8 weeks after the initial session.

How long will my brows / lips / lash line last?

Average longevity (varies by skin type, lifestyle, and color choice):

  • Brows: around 2–3.5 years
  • Lip Blush: around 2–3 years
  • Lash Line Enhancement: around 2–4 years

Softer, more natural colors fade slightly faster than very dark, saturated tones — this is normal, and we prefer it for more elegant, timeless results.

Do I always need a touch-up?

A second perfecting session at 4–6 weeks is recommended to:

  • Adjust color or saturation after seeing the healed result
  • Refine shape or symmetry if needed
  • Increase density for longer-lasting effect

Some clients are happy after one session, but most benefit from a touch-up for the best long-term result.

What will happen to my pigment in a few years?

Pigment gradually softens and fades over time. Our goal is for it to fade gracefully into softer tones rather than turning blue, red, or very uneven.

When you’re ready for a refresh, we can darken or adjust color and shape — this is usually done at a reduced refresh rate if the work was originally done at Anatomy PMU.

How do I care for my brows / lips / eyes after the procedure?

We’ll give you detailed written aftercare, but in general:

  • Keep the area clean and dry — no heavy sweating or swimming for 7–10 days
  • Apply recommended healing ointment as instructed
  • Do not pick flakes or scabs, let them fall off naturally
  • Avoid direct sun, tanning, and active skincare on the area during healing
